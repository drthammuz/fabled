# MULTIPLAYER ROGUELIKE GAME DESIGN
## Class Roles, Mechanics, Faction System & Economy

---

# CLASS ROLES & MECHANICS

## MEDIC
**Role:** Lifeline. Shows mission stakes. Enables comeback mechanics.

**Core Abilities:**
- **Mission Assessment:** Displays mission difficulty at hub (before descent). Lets team evaluate risk.
- **Revive:** Restore downed teammate to 1 HP (costs 1 use of medical supply). Once per supply. If all supplies used, no revives available.
- **Team Buff:** Poison/toxin resistance in sewer zones (negates recurring damage from contaminated water/air)

**Inventory:** 3 slots (starter Medical Supply Bag + 2 additional slots)

**Hub Role:** Assess team condition after run. Flag if team is too injured for next mission (morale/safety check).

**Economic Role:** Only class that can craft without station. Medical Scrap crafting (2-3 scrap = consumable item). Makes quick investments in healing items viable.

---

## SCOUT
**Role:** Eyes & escape. Maps unknown, relocates team safely.

**Core Abilities:**
- **Speed Boost:** Movement 20-30% faster than other classes
- **Silent Movement:** Reduced noise while sneaking. Enemies have shorter detection range when Scout is crouching.
- **Sneak Speed:** Crouch movement nearly full speed (instead of half-speed for others)
- **Tradeoff:** Reduced max health (to balance mobility advantage)

**Equipment:** 
- **Ekolod** (local map, Swedish for "recognition/survey map") 
  - Shows all rooms Scout has entered (layout/architecture)
  - Displays current vision range in real-time
  - Marks enemy/hazard icons within line-of-sight
  - Upgraded versions: larger vision range, enemy type icons, hazard warnings ahead

**Inventory:** 2 slots

**Hub Role:** Report next sector intel. Difficulty estimate, enemy types, hazard warnings. Helps team decide risk/reward for next run.

---

## SOLDIER
**Role:** Tank. Absorbs damage, deals damage. Party priority for loot.

**Core Abilities:**
- **Increased Max Health:** 50% more health than other classes
- **Increased Damage Output:** Melee + applicable ranged weapons deal more damage
- **Threat Generation:** Enemies prioritize Soldier in combat (draws aggro). Allows team repositioning.
- **Implicit Buff:** Team naturally gives Soldier first dibs on loot (because durability upgrades benefit the group most). Makes Soldier the "anchor" of economic priority.

**Equipment:** 
- **Bat** (starter weapon, upgradeable to better melee weapons: sledgehammer, electro-bat, etc.)

**Inventory:** 2 slots

**Hub Role:** Trades with faction vendors. Gets +10% better prices due to intimidation/respect. Carries heavy loot back to hub.

---

## TECH
**Role:** Lockpick & reprogrammer. Opens what others can't.

**Core Abilities:**
- **Hacking:** Skill + luck-based. Attempts to open:
  - Locked doors (opens new routes)
  - Electronic caches (access hidden loot)
  - Faction terminals (gain intel/hints)
  - Enemy drones (reprogram as temporary ally OR disable turrets/defenses)
  
- **Starter Device:** Low success rate but non-zero chance on every attempt. Fumbles risk alerting nearby enemies.

- **Device Upgrades:** Better hacker devices = higher success %, faster hacking time, lower fumble risk. Enables hacking high-level faction locks.

**Equipment:** 
- **Hacker Device** (electronic, upgradeable via Tech Scrap at Tech Stations)

**Inventory:** 2 slots

**Hub Role:** Upgrades hacker device (if Tech Station available at current camp). Decrypts looted faction data. Unlocks faction terminal intel for bonus rewards (hints to other camps, faction locations, loot locations).

# SCRAP ECONOMY SYSTEM

## Scrap Types

| Scrap Type | Role | Primary Use | Crafting Difficulty | Conversion |
|------------|------|-------------|---------------------|------------|
| **Medical Scrap** | Medic | Healing items, revive charges | EASY (2-3 scrap per item) | Sell: 10 credits per scrap |
| **Tech Scrap** | Tech | Hacker device upgrades | HARD (12-16 scrap per upgrade) | Sell: 15 credits per scrap |
| **Metal Scrap** | Soldier | Weapons, armor, durability | HARD (10-14 scrap per upgrade) | Sell: 15 credits per scrap |
| **Textile Scrap** | Scout | Speed gear, stealth upgrades, bag expansion | MEDIUM (6-8 scrap per upgrade) | Sell: 12 credits per scrap |

**Scrap Properties:**
- Each scrap piece has **weight** (takes inventory space)
- Scrap is **perishable** — if team wipes before returning to camp, all scrap is lost
- Incentivizes selling early vs. hoarding for crafting
- Forces constant resource prioritization

---

## Crafting Stations (Camp-Specific)

Not all camps have all stations. Availability depends on faction affiliation.

| Station | Crafts | Availability | Notes |
|---------|--------|--------------|-------|
| **Medical Station** | Medical Scrap items | All camps | Universal; Medic can also craft without station |
| **Tech Station** | Tech device upgrades | Tech/Military faction camps | Rare; found in research outposts, secure facilities |
| **Forge/Armory** | Soldier weapons/armor | Military faction camps | Scarce; secured locations only |
| **Workbench** | Scout speed/stealth gear | Civilian/Scavenger camps | Moderate availability |

**Impact:** Players must discover camps to access specific upgrades. Creates exploration incentive + strategic route planning.

# FACTION SYSTEM

## Overview

Multiple factions control different underground territories. Each faction has:
- Distinct ideology (from their perspective)
- Vendor/trading presence at camps
- Unique quest lines (one active per faction at a time)
- Reputation tracking (affects prices, quest access)
- Later-game: potential hostility/betrayal

## The Factions

### FACTION 1: [MILITARY/SECURITY FACTION] 
*Name TBD (authoritarian, control-focused)*

- **Territory:** Military bunkers, command centers, secured subway segments
- **Crafting:** Forge/Armory (Soldier focus)
- **Quest Type:** Defense/suppression. "Eliminate encroachment," "Secure sector," "Retrieve weapon cache"
- **Rewards:** Weapons, armor, supplies
- **Vendor:** Military-grade equipment, higher prices but better quality
- **Reputation Mechanic:** Gained by completing faction quests, helping military NPCs. Lost if you side with rival factions.

### FACTION 2: [SCAVENGER/CIVILIAN FACTION]
*Name TBD (pragmatic, survival-focused)*

- **Territory:** Civilian settlements, makeshift camps, sewer stations repurposed as shelters
- **Crafting:** Workbench (Scout/general focus)
- **Quest Type:** Supply runs. "Find medicine," "Locate food caches," "Map safe routes"
- **Rewards:** Food, shelter, maps, intel
- **Vendor:** Consumables, tools, cheaper than military but lower quality
- **Reputation Mechanic:** Gained by helping NPCs, completing low-risk missions. Lost if you're seen as too violent/reckless.

### FACTION 3: [TECH/RESEARCH FACTION]
*Name TBD (knowledge-focused, morally ambiguous)*

- **Territory:** Research labs, server farms, tech caches, ventilation systems with hidden tech
- **Crafting:** Tech Station (Tech focus)
- **Quest Type:** Data/tech retrieval. "Recover server data," "Find drone chip," "Retrieve prototype"
- **Rewards:** Upgraded tech, hacker tools, schematics
- **Vendor:** High-tech upgrades, very expensive, only accessible with high reputation
- **Reputation Mechanic:** Gained by hacking/unlocking faction terminals, retrieving their tech. Lost if you damage faction tech.

### FACTION 4: [THE SIGNED / THE SEALED]
*Name finalized: The Signed (or The Sealed)*

The augmented humans. Those who took implants/upgrades and were rejected by baseline society.

- **Perspective:** They see themselves as evolved, necessary, pragmatic. They made survival choices.
- **Outsider Perspective:** They're compromised, unreliable, potential corporate puppets. Can't trust them.
- **Territory:** Deep underground (overworld is lethal, they avoid it). Hidden enclaves, sealed tunnels, tech-integrated spaces.
- **Crafting:** Tech Station (similar to Tech faction, but different tech philosophy)
- **Quest Type:** Infiltration/networking. "Contact our people," "Disable anti-augment tech," "Retrieve implant schematics"
- **Rewards:** Implant tech, network access, special abilities
- **Vendor:** Augmentation-focused. Can sell implants/bio-tech mods (if team trusts them)
- **Reputation Mechanic:** Gained by accepting their quests, treating them as equals. Lost if you work against other factions or show bias against augmentation.
- **Special Mechanic:** If team has member who accepts Signed tech/implants, they become part-Signed. Other factions may distrust them. High risk/reward.

## Faction Quest System

### Quest Mechanics:
- **One active quest per faction at a time** — Choose which faction's quest to prioritize each run cycle
- **Underground quests:** Early access, lower risk, smaller rewards
- **Overworld quests:** Requires finding map (purchased or looted), higher difficulty, significantly better rewards

### Quest Types:

**Underground Example (Tech Faction):**
- "Retrieve server data from Sector 7 research facility"
- Descend into marked map, hack terminal, collect data chip
- Return to Tech-affiliated camp with same faction
- Collect reward (hacker upgrade + reputation)

**Overworld Example (Tech Faction):**
- "Find drone chip in overworld Sector 12"
- Requires map purchase/discovery (shows Sector 12 layout)
- Navigate surface (harder enemies, environmental hazards)
- Find chip (in cache or enemy drop)
- Return to Tech-affiliated camp to complete quest
- Reward: Advanced hacker device + significant reputation

### Faction Conflict:
- **Mid-Game:** Theft missions unlock. "Steal from rival faction camp." Higher risk, exclusive rewards.
- **Late-Game:** Faction camps may become **hostile on arrival.** Betrayal mechanic (reputation cascades). Must fight for loot instead of trade.

---

## Camp Types & Faction Affiliation

Camps are underground structures. Each has:
- **Type:** Subway station, sewer station, ventilation central, evacuation shaft
- **Faction Control:** Which faction runs it (affects vendors, quests, stations available)
- **Crafting Stations:** Depends on faction
- **NPCs:** Faction vendors, quest-givers, story characters

### Camp Discovery & Mapping:

**Without Map:**
- Team arrives at unknown camp
- Don't know faction, stations, dangers
- Must interact to discover (vendor dialogue, exploration)
- High risk, potential reward if camp is friendly

**With Map:**
- Shows faction affiliation
- Shows which crafting stations available
- Shows estimated enemy density/hazards
- Allows strategic route planning
- Maps are limited (only one team member can hold)
- Maps can be purchased at hubs or looted

---

## Faction Reputation & Late-Game Consequences

### Reputation System:
- Each faction tracks reputation independently
- Positive actions: complete their quests, help their NPCs, don't sabotage their stuff
- Negative actions: work for rival factions, steal from them, kill their members

### Late-Game Hostility (Run 20+):
- If reputation drops below threshold, camp becomes hostile
- Team must fight NPC faction members instead of trading
- Looting faction camp triggers combat
- High risk, high reward (can loot faction tech/supplies directly)
- Permanent consequence: that faction becomes permanently hostile (can't trade with them again that playthrough)

**Impact:** Forces long-term strategic choices. Can't side with everyone forever.

---

## Faction Graffiti & World Hints

As team moves through sewers toward faction camps, faction graffiti appears:
- Wormwood Collective tags → moving toward their territory
- Military markings (numbered sectors) → approaching military zone
- Tech symbols (circuit patterns) → near research facility
- Signed/Sealed glyphs (augmentation markers) → deep Signed territory

Graffiti helps team orient without revealing camp location. Builds world immersion.

# HUB ROLES SUMMARY

| Role | Hub Function |
|------|--------------|
| **Medic** | Assess team condition. Flag dangerous missions. Craft medical items from Medical Scrap (no station needed). |
| **Scout** | Report sector intel. Difficulty estimate, enemy composition, hazards. Helps team choose next run strategy. |
| **Soldier** | Trade with faction vendors (10% price discount). Carries heavy loot. Negotiates with faction NPCs (gets better prices/more options than other classes). |
| **Tech** | Upgrade hacker device (if Tech Station available at current camp). Decrypt looted faction data. Unlock faction terminal intel for bonus rewards/hints. |

# GAME FLOW SUMMARY

## Per-Run Cycle:
1. **Hub Phase:** Team selects next map/faction quest. Medic assesses risk. Purchases map if desired (only one holder).
2. **Descent:** Team enters underground map (sewer, subway, vent, etc.). 
3. **Exploration:** Scout maps layout. Tech hacks caches/doors. Soldier tanks combat. Medic keeps team alive.
4. **Combat:** Enemies vary by faction territory (Signed drones, Military guards, etc.). Enemies prioritize Soldier.
5. **Looting:** Scrap collected (weight-based, limited inventory).
6. **Quest Completion (if applicable):** Find objective, return to correct faction camp for reward.
7. **Return to Hub:** Team trades scrap, buys items from vendors, assigns loot priority to Soldier.

## Progression:
- **Early Game (Runs 1-5):** Underground only. Learn mechanics. Build reputation.
- **Mid Game (Runs 6-15):** Unlock overworld maps. Faction theft quests appear. Reputation matters.
- **Late Game (Runs 16+):** Faction hostility possible. High-risk/high-reward playstyle emerges. Team must choose faction alignment.

# DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

**Core Tensions:**
- Individual class power vs. team dependency
- Immediate vendor purchases vs. long-term crafting investment
- Faction loyalty vs. opportunistic theft
- Safe underground vs. dangerous but rewarding overworld
- Risk (permadeath) vs. reward (scrap/loot)

**Each decision forces tradeoffs. No dominant strategy. Emergent replayability.**
