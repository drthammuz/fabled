# Fabled — game pitch (author statements)

**Status:** Design notes captured from author conversation (2026-06-23)  
**Note:** Structured summary of what the author stated. Not implementation spec.

---

## Release structure

- Ships initially as a **Repo-style game** (co-op extraction).
- Critics say **R.E.P.O. has no end** — this game **does not** have “an end to collect money” in that sense; it has a storyline arc.
- **Early release:** storyline kept **simple** — **Terminator 2–style Cyberdyne systems / Zion in The Matrix** (underground resistance vs megacorp).
- When **cash starts flowing in**, work continues toward **full release**.
- The **underground start becomes a prologue**:
  - **Mandatory once per player** (must carry a player through if they haven’t done it).
  - **Optional replay** to improve **base stats**.
- Then comes **campaign** — when the **overworld is introduced** (Chapter 2).
- Given a **successful campaign / Chapter 2** → **Chapter 3: open world / MMORPG**.

---

## World and tone

- **Cyberpunk theme** (industrial underground substrate).
- **Faction-based camps** on the run graph.
- Camps are **inhabited**, **integrated into maps**.
- **No load time** — intended to increase **immersiveness**.

---

## Professions and factions

- **Four professions**; each **faction is biased toward one profession**.
- Creates **choices between players** (party composition).
- **Synth tech** and **faction affiliation** create **unique character arcs / subspecialties**.

### Scout

- From the start: **only scout has the map** during the **first part of the game**.
- **Sneaks significantly better**.

### Soldier

- **Only the soldier can really take on enemies in the beginning**.

### Medic

- **“Sense danger” ability** — initially some **unreliable red buzzing** when enemies are close.
- During the **first map that leads to priesthood**:
  - A few random occurrences of **premonitions**.
  - **Premonition feature:** sees a **red/black copy of an enemy ~10 seconds before it appears**.
  - Each enemy has a **ghost copy projected 10 seconds ahead** of where it’s going.
  - Ghost visible **only when medic premonition is active**.
  - **First camp** informs that **medic implants are the only sanctioned synth-tech**.
  - **First medic quest** gives **level 1 premonition**.
- Example of **class-specific utility** — there will be **RPG / character progression** distinct from similar games.

### Tech

- From the start: at least some chance to **open caches and doors** with **semi skill-point–based, semi human-skill–based hacking minigames**.
- Useful if he or others buy **over-expensive but over-powered implants**:
  - **Cheaper if he builds some components**.
  - Can **learn to install** implants too.
- Option to **overcharge electrical weapons or cybernetics powers** with **increasingly large risk**.
- **Highest potential power early on**, but **greater risk**.
- Good as **last man standing** when it’s worth taking such risks.
- Party keeps him **alive for hacking**.

---

## Multiclass and party composition

- **No medic in team → no medic quest** (medic quest requires medic).
- The **tech guy can get medic implants as a second class**.

---

## Camps, quests, cooperation

- Each **camp can give a quest** that must be **returned to the same faction** for a **reward for that player**.
- Warrants **cooperation** (party routing / escort / shared goals).

---

## Faction reputation

- Factions have **reputation values**.
- Choosing to install **unsanctioned neuralinks / cybernetics** turns **other factions against you**.
- Powerful, but **expensive in money** and **huge rep loss** with certain factions.
- **Outlaws care but not as much as priesthood**.
- To survive **increasing difficulty**, **choices have to be made** — **some faction needs to be sacrificed**.

### Hostile camp / faction turn

- Eventually a camp the player approaches is **suddenly not friendly anymore**:
  - **Hub is empty**.
  - **New music**.
- Upon **leaving hub**, player enters a **new type of level**:
  - **Only that faction** for the level.
  - Suddenly **fight that faction**.
- If **successful**: **greater rewards**, but **tanked rep with that faction** (possibly **increased rep with other factions**).

### High-level quests

- Example: **kill a bishop** — **act-ending kind of difficulty / boss fight / needs prep**.
